Shortly after graduating summa cum laude from Southern Utah University, Cole Robinson, M.D., attended the University of Utah Medical School. While pursuing his medical degree, Dr. Robinson completed a pediatric residency at Loma Linda University Children’s Hospital in Loma Linda, California. Upon completion, he began a second residency in physical medicine and rehabilitation. Dr. Robinson then completed a fellowship in interventional pain management.

While in training, Dr. Robinson conducted research on spinal cord injuries and related central pain states, which led to published articles in multiple medical journals. His extensive training resulted in board certifications in three distinct medical specialties.

Dr. Robinson brings a unique perspective to Southwest Spine and Pain Center and Central Utah, providing specialized pain management care to Sevier and Sanpete counties. He has a special interest in helping improve the lives of his patients using the latest minimally invasive therapies and treatments. Dr. Robinson grew up in Parowan, Utah, and is happy to live and serve in the area. He enjoys traveling, being outdoors, camping, reading, and attending University of Utah sporting events. He is married and has five children.

Dr. Frieden is board-certified in anesthesiology and pain medicine and provides advanced pain management care for patients across Southern Utah. His training includes medical school at Ross University School of Medicine, residency at Saint Louis University Hospital, and fellowship training in pain management at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center affiliated with Harvard University.

With a focus on interventional pain medicine, Dr. Frieden works with patients to identify treatment options that support function, mobility, and quality of life.

After graduating from Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine, Dr. Jon Obray completed fellowship training in interventional pain medicine at Mayo Clinic. Following this influential training, he served Mayo Medical Center as the Medical Director of the pain clinic, and previously served as Medical Director of Neurosciences at Dixie Regional Medical Center. Dr. Obray is also actively involved in research as a clinical research investigator. Dedicated to the treatment of spine conditions and painful disorders, Dr. Obray’s special interests include minimally-invasive technologies to improve the lives of his patients.
